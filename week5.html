Robinson Crusoe – Building a Raft

In this part of the story, Crusoe stops waiting and decides to take action. He builds a raft from ship materials so he can carry supplies to shore.

Why He Builds the Raft

  • He realizes sitting and hoping will not help.
  • He needs food, tools, and weapons to survive.
  • The raft is the only way to move heavy items safely.

Materials He Uses

  • Large wooden spars and spare yards
  • Planks and boards for the surface
  • A spare topmast cut into pieces
  • Ropes to tie everything together

What He Loads First

Food

  • Bread and rice
  • Dutch cheese
  • Dried goat meat
  • Grain (later ruined by rats)

Tools

  • Carpenter’s chest
  • Saws, hammer, and axe
  • He values tools more than gold

Weapons

  • Two guns and two pistols
  • Powder and shot
  • Old swords

Challenges on the Sea

  • The raft almost tips when it hits shallow water.
  • He must hold the chests to stop them from falling.
  • He waits for the tide to rise before moving again.

Why He Succeeds

  • The sea is calm.
  • The tide moves toward land.
  • A small wind helps guide the raft.

Final Decision

Crusoe chooses to stay close to the coast. He hopes he might see a ship one day, but for now his priority is survival and preparation.
